# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is Billy Rovzar and the Lemon Studios producing team. They use the dashboard as a working slate, often reviewing many screenplays in one sitting on a desktop monitor. Their job is to identify promising material quickly, understand why a script scored as it did, record producer judgment, compare candidates, and move from AI coverage to a human decision without exporting to another tool.

## Product Purpose

The dashboard turns a large screenplay library into an actionable development slate. It ingests scripts, runs the V9 multi-reader analysis, exposes evidence and commercial signals, and helps producers filter, rank, compare, review, share, and resolve ingestion problems. Success means a producer can understand the state of the slate at a glance and reach a defensible next action with minimal friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, decisive, cinematic. The interface should feel like a serious studio instrument: information-dense without feeling cramped, confident without being loud, and polished without becoming decorative. Copy is direct and operational.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI dashboards with glowing gradients, glass panels, decorative atmosphere, or novelty motion.
- Analytics templates that compress labels until charts become unreadable.
- Editorial layouts that prioritize serif styling over scanning and repeated action.
- Card grids where every section floats independently and visual hierarchy disappears.
- Interfaces that expose problems without giving the producer a clear next action.

## Design Principles

1. Decision before decoration: every surface should clarify the next producer decision.
2. Calm shell, loud data: navigation stays quiet while scores, verdicts, and exceptions carry visual weight.
3. Readable at a glance: no essential label, value, or action may depend on tiny type or hover alone.
4. Dense but ordered: preserve the power of a professional workbench while grouping related controls and evidence clearly.
5. Human judgment is primary: AI findings support producer taste and decisions rather than presenting themselves as authority.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for text contrast, focus visibility, control labeling, and keyboard operation. Support reduced motion. Do not encode status by color alone. Essential chart values and labels must remain legible at desktop and mobile sizes, with alternate text or summaries when a chart cannot carry every label cleanly.
