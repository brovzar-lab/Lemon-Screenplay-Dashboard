## ðŸŽ“ Understanding Story Grid Elements

### Five-Leaf Clover
Not just "genre" - it's 6 dimensions:
- **Time:** Does character change (Arc) or stay the same (Flat)?
- **Reality:** Realistic or fantastical world?
- **Style:** Serious, satirical, or ironic tone?
- **External Content:** The "marketing genre" (Action, Thriller, Love, etc.)
- **Structure:** Archplot (classic), Miniplot (art house), or Antiplot (experimental)?
- **Internal Content:** Character's internal journey (Worldview, Morality, Status)

### Objects of Desire
- **Conscious (A-Story):** External goal driving the plot
  - Example: "Stop the killer" in a Thriller
- **Subconscious (B-Story):** Internal need driving character arc
  - Example: "Find redemption" in the same Thriller

**Best stories:** A-Story and B-Story collide at Crisis

### Value Progression
The emotional journey on your genre's spectrum:
- **Thriller:** Life â†’ Unconsciousness â†’ Death â†’ Damnation
- **Love:** Hate â†’ Indifference â†’ Like â†’ Love
- **Action:** Weakness â†’ Strength â†’ Power

**Q: Can I skip Story Grid?**

**Q: How long does AI Analysis take?**
A: 2-3 minutes. The AI analyzes your logline and suggests Story Grid elements.

**Q: Can I change Story Grid elements later?**
A: Yes! Return to Stage 1, Step 3 anytime. Just regenerate your synopsis afterward.

**Q: What if I don't know my genre?**
A: Use the **Genre Guide** button. It explains each genre's conventions, obligatory scenes, and value spectrum.

**Q: Do I need all 6 genres in the Five-Leaf Clover?**
A: Minimum: External Content Genre. More complete = better AI synopsis.
