# Enneagram Character Examples from Film & Television

Reference guide showing how each Enneagram type manifests in iconic film and television characters.

## Type 1 - The Perfectionist

**Atticus Finch** (*To Kill a Mockingbird*)
- Driven by moral righteousness and justice
- Fears corruption and moral failure
- Arc: Maintains integrity despite community pressure

**Mary Poppins** (*Mary Poppins*)
- Perfectionist about proper behavior and manners
- Believes everything has a right way to be done
- Integration: Shows Type 7 joy and spontaneity

**Miranda Priestly** (*The Devil Wears Prada*)
- Unhealthy Type 1: Critical, impossible standards
- Fears mediocrity and being wrong
- Rarely shows growth arc (remains in stress)

## Type 2 - The Helper

**Samwise Gamgee** (*The Lord of the Rings*)
- Selflessly devoted to helping Frodo
- Fears being unloved or useless
- Healthy Type 2: Gives without expectation

**Molly Weasley** (*Harry Potter*)
- Nurturing, protective, people-pleasing
- Stress reaction: Becomes controlling (Type 8)
- Core fear: Loved ones being harmed

**Glenn Close's character** (*Fatal Attraction*)
- Unhealthy Type 2: Manipulative when love is rejected
- Extreme stress response showing Type 8 aggression

## Type 3 - The Achiever

**Jordan Belfort** (*The Wolf of Wall Street*)
- Driven by success and admiration
- Unhealthy Type 3: Image over authenticity
- Devolution arc into Type 9 apathy after crash

**Andy Sachs** (*The Devil Wears Prada*)
- Achiever seeking success in fashion
- Arc: Learns to integrate Type 6 values (loyalty to self)
- Chooses authenticity over achievement

**Don Draper** (*Mad Men*)
- Success-driven, image-conscious
- Fears worthlessness, hides true identity
- Struggles between achievement and authenticity

## Type 4 - The Individualist

**Edward Scissorhands** (*Edward Scissorhands*)
- Longs to be understood and belong
- Fears being ordinary or defective
- Tragic arc: Isolation reinforces uniqueness

**Amélie Poulain** (*Amélie*)
- Creative, imaginative, slightly melancholic
- Integration: Moves to Type 1 discipline (acts on love)
- Overcomes self-absorption

**Hamlet** (*Hamlet*)
- Introspective, brooding, self-absorbed
- Stuck in melancholy and inaction
- Classic unhealthy Type 4

## Type 5 - The Investigator

**Sherlock Holmes** (*Sherlock*)
- Analytical, detached, knowledge-driven
- Fears incompetence and being overwhelmed by emotion
- Stress: Type 7 scattered behavior (cases as distraction)

**Dr. Temperance Brennan** (*Bones*)
- Logical, independent, emotionally detached
- Arc: Integrates Type 8 confidence in relationships
- Gradually learns emotional connection

**Bruce Banner** (*The Avengers*)
- Intellectual, withdrawn, isolated
- Stress point: Hulk represents Type 7's impulsive chaos
- Fears being overwhelmed by emotions

## Type 6 - The Loyalist

**Ron Weasley** (*Harry Potter*)
- Loyal friend, anxious, seeks security
- Fears abandonment and being alone
- Stress: Shows Type 3 competitive jealousy

**Marlin** (*Finding Nemo*)
- Anxious, worst-case scenario thinking
- Driven by need to keep son safe
- Integration: Learns Type 9 trust and letting go

**Frodo Baggins** (*The Lord of the Rings*)
- Dutiful, anxious about responsibility
- Seeks guidance and support
- Stress arc: Becomes increasingly paranoid

## Type 7 - The Enthusiast

**Peter Pan** (*Peter Pan*)
- Eternal optimist, avoids pain of growing up
- Fears being trapped or experiencing pain
- Refuses integration (never grows up)

**Ariel** (*The Little Mermaid*)
- Adventurous, impulsive, seeking excitement
- Avoids pain of her current life
- Integration: Type 5 depth (willing to sacrifice for love)

**Jack Sparrow** (*Pirates of the Caribbean*)
- Scattered, pleasure-seeking, avoids commitment
- Uses humor and adventure to escape pain
- Healthy aspects: Clever, resourceful

## Type 8 - The Challenger

**Tony Soprano** (*The Sopranos*)
- Controlling, confrontational, protective
- Fears being controlled or showing vulnerability
- Rare moments of Type 2 integration (caring for ducks)

**Ellen Ripley** (*Alien*)
- Protective, confronts danger directly
- Healthy Type 8: Strong without being domineering
- Integration: Shows Type 2 care for Newt

**Miranda Bailey** (*Grey's Anatomy*)
- Authoritative, direct, protective of residents
- Fear: Being weak or losing control
- Shows both healthy and stressed expressions

## Type 9 - The Peacemaker

**Bilbo Baggins** (*The Hobbit*)
- Peace-loving, avoids conflict
- Forced into adventure disrupting harmony
- Integration: Shows Type 3 assertiveness

**Pam Beesly** (*The Office*)
- Conflict-avoidant, goes along to get along
- Arc: Integrates Type 3 (pursues art, stands up for self)
- Fear: Conflict with Jim or office harmony disrupted

**Forrest Gump** (*Forrest Gump*)
- Easygoing, accepts life as it comes
- Healthy Type 9: Creates harmony around him
- Never stressed; maintains peaceful presence

## Team Dynamics Examples

### *Ocean's Eleven* Team
- **Danny Ocean (Type 3)**: Achiever leader, image-conscious
- **Rusty Ryan (Type 7)**: Enthusiastic, quick-thinking
- **Linus Caldwell (Type 6)**: Loyal, anxious for approval
- **Basher Tarr (Type 8)**: Confrontational, direct

Storming phase: Type 6 Linus questions Type 3 Danny's plan
Performing phase: Each type contributes unique strength

### *The Avengers*
- **Captain America (Type 1)**: Moral perfectionist
- **Iron Man (Type 3)**: Achievement-driven, image-conscious
- **Hulk (Type 5→7 stress)**: Withdrawn scientist, chaotic under stress
- **Thor (Type 8)**: Confrontational, protective
- **Black Widow (Type 6)**: Loyal, strategic

Natural conflicts: Type 1 vs Type 3 (morality vs pragmatism)

### *Friends*
- **Monica (Type 1)**: Perfectionist, controlling
- **Phoebe (Type 4)**: Individualist, authentic
- **Rachel (Type 3)**: Achievement in career/image
- **Joey (Type 7)**: Fun-loving, avoids commitment
- **Chandler (Type 6)**: Anxious, loyal
- **Ross (Type 5)**: Intellectual, somewhat detached

Demonstrates how different types navigate same situations differently

## Using These Examples

1. **Identify the type**: Watch for core fear and desire
2. **Track the arc**: Does character evolve or devolve?
3. **Note stress/integration**: When do they show other type behaviors?
4. **Study team dynamics**: How do different types conflict and complement?
5. **Apply to your work**: Use patterns, not exact copies
