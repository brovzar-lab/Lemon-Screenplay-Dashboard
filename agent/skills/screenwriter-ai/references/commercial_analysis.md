## 1. The "Is It a Movie?" Test

Not every good story is a good MOVIE. Test the premise against cinema-specific requirements.

### The 7 Requirements

| # | Requirement | Test | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|---|------------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Visual Promise | Can you describe 3 striking images from this film? | If you can't SEE it, it may be a novel |
| 2 | Time Compression | Can this story be told in 90-120 minutes? | If it needs 5 hours, it's a series |
| 3 | Active Protagonist | Does the lead DRIVE the action? | Passive leads = box office poison |
| 4 | Clear Stakes | What specific thing is at risk? | Vague stakes = disengaged audience |
| 5 | Emotional Clarity | What ONE emotion dominates? | Multiple competing emotions = muddy |
| 6 | Ticking Clock | Is there urgency (literal or implied)? | No urgency = no tension |
| 7 | Satisfying Ending | Can you deliver a cathartic conclusion? | Ambiguous endings need earned setup |

**Scoring:** 5-7 passes = strong film premise. 3-4 = needs work. Under 3 = may not be a movie.

## 2. Budget Tier Analysis

Know what your screenplay costs before you write it.

### Budget Tiers and What They Mean

**Micro-Budget ($500K — $5M)**

- **Constraints:** 1-3 locations, <10 speaking roles, no VFX, no stars
- **Strengths:** High concept overcomes low budget, creative freedom
- **Examples:** *Get Out*, *Paranormal Activity*, *Whiplash*
- **Writing Strategy:** Concept is everything. One location, one weekend, life-or-death stakes
- **Buyer:** Indies, micro-studios, streaming originals

**Low-Budget ($5M — $15M)**

- **Constraints:** Limited locations, modest cast, minimal VFX
- **Strengths:** Can attract one recognizable actor, genre-friendly
- **Examples:** *A Quiet Place*, *The Menu*, *Saltburn*
- **Writing Strategy:** Contained thriller/horror, sharp ensemble, one star role + strong supporting
- **Buyer:** A24, Blumhouse, Neon, streaming

**Mid-Budget ($15M — $60M)**

- **Constraints:** Need star attachment, clear marketing hook
- **Strengths:** Real budget for production value, can be serious or genre
- **Examples:** *Knives Out*, *Top Gun: Maverick* (started here), *The Banshees of Inisherin*
- **Writing Strategy:** Write a role an A-lister wants. Franchise potential helps
- **Buyer:** Mini-majors, studios for prestige, streaming for star packages

**Tentpole ($60M — $300M+)**

- **Constraints:** Must be four-quadrant, usually IP-based, franchise potential
- **Strengths:** Massive marketing spend, global release, cultural event
- **Examples:** MCU, *Dune*, *Barbie*, *Oppenheimer*
- **Writing Strategy:** Build a world, not just a movie. IP adaptation or original event
- **Buyer:** Major studios only

### Budget Impact Checklist

Before writing, assess what your screenplay requires:

- [ ] Number of unique locations (each = money)
- [ ] Period vs. contemporary (period = 20-50% more)
- [ ] VFX requirements (how many shots, complexity)
- [ ] Stunts/action sequences (coordination = cost)
- [ ] Cast size (each speaking role = SAG minimum)
- [ ] Animals/children (special considerations = cost + time)
- [ ] Night shoots (premium crew rates)
- [ ] Water/weather (most expensive production elements)

## 3. Casting Appeal Strategy

**A-list actors choose roles that offer:**

### The ACTOR Test

| Letter | Question | What Actors Want |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **A** | Agency | Does the character drive the story? |
| **C** | Complexity | Is there emotional range to display? |
| **T** | Transformation | Does the character change meaningfully? |
| **O** | Opportunities | Are there showcase moments (monologues, breakdowns)? |
| **R** | Range | Does the role stretch beyond their usual type? |

### Writing for Castability

- **The Showcase Scene:** Every lead needs at least ONE scene that would make an actor's reel
- **Against-Type Potential:** Write roles that let funny actors be serious, action stars be vulnerable
- **The "I've Never Seen This" Factor:** What makes this character unlike anything the actor has played?
- **Supporting Role Bait:** Strong supporting roles attract stars willing to do smaller parts

**AI Prompt:**

```
Analyze this character for casting appeal: [CHARACTER DESCRIPTION]

SHOWCASE MOMENT: What scene would actors use for their awards reel?
AGAINST-TYPE POTENTIAL: Which type of actor would this stretch?
RANGE DEMANDS: What emotions must the actor convincingly portray?
PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS: Any transformations, skills, physicality needed?
STAR TARGETS: 5 specific actors who'd respond to this role and why
```

## 4. Marketing Hook Assessment

Can the marketing team sell this movie?

### The 4 Marketing Pillars

**1. The One-Sheet (Poster)**

- Can you create a compelling poster from one image?
- Is the genre immediately clear?
- Does the title intrigue?
- Test: Describe the poster in one sentence

**2. The Trailer (2.5 minutes)**

- Are there 5-7 visually dynamic moments to cut?
- Can you build a three-act mini-story in the trailer?
- Is there a money shot? (The image everyone shares)
- Is there a killer line of dialogue for the end card?

**3. The Tagline (One Line)**

- Sum up the emotional promise in 6-10 words
- Examples: "In space, no one can hear you scream" / "Be afraid. Be very afraid."
- Test: Would someone repeat this to a friend?

**4. The Social Hook (Shareability)**

- Is there a twist people want to discuss? (Spoiler culture = free marketing)
- Are there meme-worthy moments?
- Does it tap into a current cultural conversation?
- Test: Would this trend on social media?

## 5. Genre Marketplace Positioning

### Genre Performance Analysis

**AI Prompt:**

```
Analyze the current marketplace for [GENRE] films:

SATURATION: How crowded is this genre right now?
AUDIENCE APPETITE: Growing, stable, or declining interest?
RECENT PERFORMANCE: Top 5 performers and bottom 5 in last 3 years
STREAMING VS. THEATRICAL: Where is this genre performing better?
FESTIVAL TRAJECTORY: Is this genre winning awards?
INTERNATIONAL APPEAL: Does this genre travel globally?
EMERGING SUBGENRES: What new hybrids are gaining traction?
WHITE SPACE: What type of [GENRE] is missing from the market?
```

### Positioning Strategy

**Three positioning approaches:**

1. **Genre-Pure** — Satisfy every convention, compete on execution
   - Risk: Crowded field. Reward: Clear audience

2. **Genre-Elevated** — Use genre framework but aim higher (A24 model)
   - Risk: "Too smart" for genre fans, "too genre" for drama fans. Reward: Critical + commercial

3. **Genre-Hybrid** — Combine two genres for freshness
   - Risk: Confused marketing. Reward: Untapped audience

## 6. Distribution Strategy

### Theatrical vs. Streaming Assessment

| Factor | Favors Theatrical | Favors Streaming |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| Visual spectacle | ✅ Big screen sells | ❌ Loses impact |
| Star power | ✅ Opens wide | ✅ Algorithm boost |
| Genre (Horror, Action) | ✅ Communal experience | ⚠️ Depends on concept |
| Intimate drama | ❌ Soft opening | ✅ Discovery over time |
| High concept | ✅ Marketing hook clear | ✅ Thumbnail appeal |
| Niche audience | ❌ Can't fill theaters | ✅ Finds its audience |
| Franchise potential | ✅ Box office momentum | ✅ Series expansion |
| Awards play | ✅ Theater run required | ⚠️ Changing rules |

### Festival Strategy

- **Sundance** — Discovery, indie darlings, social issue films
- **Cannes** — Auteur prestige, international market
- **Venice** — Awards season launchpad
- **Toronto (TIFF)** — Audience award = Oscar predictor
- **SXSW** — Genre, comedy, midnight sections
- **Tribeca** — Documentaries, NYC stories, cultural relevance

## 7. The Commercial Scorecard

Rate the screenplay 1-5 on each dimension:

```
CONCEPT STRENGTH      [  /5] — Is the logline irresistible?
GENRE CLARITY         [  /5] — Is the genre clear and satisfied?
PROTAGONIST APPEAL    [  /5] — Would a star want this role?
AUDIENCE EMOTION      [  /5] — Will they FEEL something?
VISUAL IMPACT         [  /5] — Is this cinematic?
MARKET TIMING         [  /5] — Is the world ready for this?
MARKETABILITY         [  /5] — Can marketing sell this?
BUDGET FEASIBILITY    [  /5] — Can it be made at the right price?
FRANCHISE POTENTIAL   [  /5] — Could there be a sequel/series?
GLOBAL APPEAL         [  /5] — Does it work internationally?

TOTAL                 [  /50]
```

**Scoring Guide:**

- **40-50**: Highly commercial. Package aggressively
- **30-39**: Strong potential. Address weak spots
- **20-29**: Needs significant commercial strengthening
- **Under 20**: May be a passion project, not a commercial play

**AI Prompt:**

```
Score this screenplay concept on the Commercial Scorecard: [CONCEPT]

Rate each of the 10 dimensions 1-5 with specific justification.
For any score below 3, prescribe a concrete fix to improve it.
Provide an overall commercial assessment and recommended 
distribution strategy.
```
