## 1. Genre Intelligence Research

Know the rules before breaking them.

### Genre Convention Matrix

**For any genre, research these 5 dimensions:**

| Dimension | What to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Conventions | Required story elements | Meeting audience expectations |
| Obligatory Scenes | Scenes the genre demands | Satisfying genre contract |
| Recent Hits (3 years) | What's working NOW | Market currency |
| Recent Misses | What flopped and why | Avoid mistakes |
| Innovation Zone | Where conventions are being bent | Opportunity space |

**AI Research Prompt:**

```
Research the [GENRE] genre for feature films. Provide:

CONVENTIONS: The 8-10 elements audiences expect
OBLIGATORY SCENES: The must-have scenes for this genre
RECENT HITS (last 3 years): 5 films, their hook, and box office
RECENT MISSES (last 3 years): 3 films that underperformed and why
INNOVATION OPPORTUNITIES: Where is this genre ready for reinvention?
AUDIENCE PROFILE: Who watches this genre? Demographics + psychographics
TONE SPECTRUM: The range from lightest to darkest in this genre
```

### Subgenre Deep-Dives

Most genres have 5-10 subgenres with distinct conventions:

- **Thriller**: Psychological, Political, Techno, Erotic, Legal, Conspiracy
- **Horror**: Slasher, Supernatural, Psychological, Body, Folk, Cosmic
- **Comedy**: Rom-Com, Dark, Buddy, Satire, Farce, Coming-of-Age
- **Action**: Heist, Revenge, Survival, Espionage, Martial Arts, Disaster

Research the specific subgenre, not just the parent genre.

## 2. Market Landscape Analysis

### The Buyer Landscape

**Research what each distribution tier wants:**

| Tier | Typical Budget | What Sells | Current Appetite |
|------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Streaming (Netflix, Apple, Amazon) | $5-200M | IP, star packages, four-quadrant | High-concept genre |
| Major Studios (Disney, WB, Universal) | $50-300M | Franchise, sequel, IP adaptation | Known brands |
| Mini-Majors (Lionsgate, A24, Neon) | $5-30M | Auteur-driven, genre-elevated | Festival heat + commercial hook |
| Independents | $1-10M | High concept, contained, castable | Fresh voices, limited locations |

**AI Market Research Prompt:**

```
Analyze the current market for a [GENRE] screenplay with 
the concept: [CONCEPT]

COMP TITLES: 5 similar films from last 5 years with performance data
BUYER TARGETS: Which studios/streamers would be most interested and why
BUDGET TIER: Realistic budget range for this concept
TALENT TARGETS: 5 actors who would want this lead role and why
TIMING: Is the market hungry or saturated for this type of story?
PACKAGING STRATEGY: What elements would make this a package?
```

### Comp Title Analysis

**Every screenplay needs 2-3 comp titles.** Research them thoroughly:

1. **Tone comp** — "It FEELS like [film]"
2. **Structure comp** — "It's STRUCTURED like [film]"
3. **Market comp** — "It PERFORMED like [film]"

**Important:** Comps should be successful films from the last 5-7 years that buyers recognize but that the screenplay isn't directly imitating.

## 3. Subject Matter Research

### Building Authenticity

**Six layers of subject matter research:**

1. **Surface Details** — Terminology, uniforms, locations, tools of the trade
2. **Daily Rhythms** — What does a typical day look like for this character?
3. **Professional Protocols** — Procedures, hierarchies, rules (written and unwritten)
4. **Insider Knowledge** — What does only someone in this world know?
5. **Emotional Reality** — What does it FEEL like to live in this world?
6. **Conflict Zones** — Where do tensions naturally emerge?

**AI Research Prompt:**

```
Research [PROFESSION/SUBCULTURE/WORLD] for a feature screenplay.

VOCABULARY: 20 terms/jargon an insider would use naturally
DAILY LIFE: Typical day, routines, rituals
HIERARCHY: Power structure, ranks, pecking order
INSIDER DETAILS: 10 things only someone in this world would know
EMOTIONAL TERRAIN: Common frustrations, fears, satisfactions
NATURAL CONFLICTS: Where do tensions arise in this world?
VISUAL TEXTURE: What does this world LOOK like? Sensory details
COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS: What do movies usually get wrong?
```

### Verification Protocol

AI research provides a strong foundation but requires verification for:

- **Legal procedures** — Specific jurisdiction matters
- **Medical details** — Accuracy saves credibility
- **Military protocols** — Branch-specific differences
- **Technical processes** — Step-by-step accuracy
- **Cultural specifics** — Sensitivity and authenticity

**Rule:** If it would embarrass you in front of an expert, verify it.

## 4. Character Authenticity Research

### Occupational Research

**AI Prompt:**

```
I'm writing a character who is a [OCCUPATION]. Research:

HOW THEY TALK: Speech patterns, vocabulary, verbal tics
HOW THEY THINK: Decision-making framework, priorities, biases
HOW THEY CARRY THEMSELVES: Body language, physicality, presence
WHAT THEY FEAR: Professional and personal anxieties
WHAT THEY HIDE: Common secrets in this profession
STATUS MARKERS: How insiders signal hierarchy/competence
DEFORMATION PROFESSIONNELLE: How the job warps their personality
DAILY IRRITATIONS: Small frustrations specific to the role
```

### Dialect and Voice Research

**AI Prompt:**

```
Research speech patterns for a character from [REGION/BACKGROUND]:

SYNTAX: Sentence structure patterns (short/long, direct/indirect)
VOCABULARY: Region/class-specific word choices (5-10 examples)
RHYTHM: Pacing and cadence (staccato? flowing? fragmented?)
EXPRESSIONS: Idioms, slang, characteristic phrases
AVOIDANCES: What this person would NEVER say
CODE-SWITCHING: How they adjust speech in different contexts
```

## 5. Historical & Period Research

### Period Film Research Framework

**AI Prompt:**

```
Research [TIME PERIOD] for a [GENRE] screenplay set in [LOCATION]:

VISUAL WORLD: Architecture, fashion, technology, transportation
SOCIAL NORMS: What was acceptable? Taboo? Aspirational?
DAILY LIFE: Food, work, entertainment, communication
LANGUAGE: Period-appropriate speech patterns (without overdoing it)
POWER STRUCTURES: Who had power? How was it exercised?
EMERGING TENSIONS: What was changing in society at this time?
ANACHRONISM TRAPS: Common mistakes films make about this era
CINEMATIC OPPORTUNITIES: Visually striking period details for the camera
```

### Historical Accuracy vs. Dramatic License

**Research to determine the line:**

- What historical facts are the foundation? (Don't change these)
- What gaps exist in the record? (Creative freedom zone)
- What can be compressed/combined for drama? (Time, characters, events)
- What would historians flag if changed? (Risk assessment)

## 6. The Story Bible Format

Compile all research into a single reference document.

**Story Bible Structure:**

```markdown
# [PROJECT TITLE] — Story Bible

## World
- Setting, time period, locations
- Rules (physical, social, supernatural)
- Visual tone and palette

## Characters
- Full profiles for all speaking roles
- Relationship map
- Character voices (speech samples)

## Subject Matter
- Technical details and terminology
- Insider knowledge
- Verification notes (confirmed vs. assumed)

## Genre & Market
- Genre conventions to satisfy
- Comp titles and positioning
- Target audience profile
- Budget tier and production notes

## Research Sources
- Primary sources consulted
- Expert consultations needed
- Verification gaps to fill
```

**The Story Bible is a living document** — update it throughout the writing process as new research needs emerge.
